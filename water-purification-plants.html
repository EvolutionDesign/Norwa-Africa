Water purification plants

Standard stages of purification:

1. Pretreatment water purification

Pretreatment is used to assit in the work for reverse osmosis purification and it can have the following stages depending on the feed water quality:

  • Oxidation of iron & iron removal
  • Flocculantion
  • pH adjustment
  • Sediment removal
  • Organic chemicals, chlorine & odours
  • Water softening
  • Ultrafiltration
  • Biofouling prevention

  • Membranes & vessels

2. Reverse osmosis water purification

Compared to traditional filtration technologies that rely on a screen or filter to remove particles, reverse osmosis (RO) is a pressure-driven separation process that employs a semipermeable membrane and the principles of crossflow filtration.Reverse osmosis water treatment provides the finest level of filtration & therefore a highly effective process for removing contaminants such as:

  • Endotoxins/pyrogens.
  • Insecticides/pesticides.
  • Herbicides.
  • Antibiotics.
  • Nitrates.
  • Sugars.
  • Soluble salts.
  • Metal ions.

3. Post-treatment water purification

It is suitable for the removal and deactivation of micro-oragnisms and bacteria thus reducing the risk of water borne diseases. Post-treatment stages depends on the method of storage such as:

  • Biofouling prevention
  • UV Filtration
  • Disinfection

Operating parameters

Raw water quality:

  • TDS level of upto 2000 ppm for the low brackish water
  • TDS level of upto 5000 ppm for the medium brackish water
  • TDS level of upto 8000 ppm for the sea water
  • No chlorine
  • Chloride level should be less than 350 ppm
  • Total hardness < 200 ppm & no suspended solids
