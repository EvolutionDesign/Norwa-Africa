400 Litres per day salty or borehole water purification for domestic use

Treatment process

Reverse osmosis salt water purification

1.Raw tap water first flows through a sediment filter to remove dirt, rust other solid objects.
2.The water then flows into a carbon filter that takes out 98% of the chlorine and organic chemicals, herbicides, pesticides.
3.Next the water flows through Block activated carbon a fine sediment reduction for removal of odor, taste, cyst sand turbidity that may have passed second stage.
4. Next, reverse osmosis membrane separates 70-90% of the dissolved contaminants from the water molecules. These impurities are drained down the drain producing the final product “pure water.”
5.This water is stored in a reservoir tank typically located underneath the kitchen sink.
6.When you open the tap the water is filtered one last time with a polishing filter.
7. Finally the water is sterilized through ultraviolet rays to kill all virus, microorganisms before it reaches your glass.

Installation of the reverse osmosis machine

The unit can be placed flat or upright and on top, inside or under the cabinet. Feed water connection should be close as possible to the unit. However, if due to space or other limitations, this unit can be placed where it is convenient. The location for installing the unit will require easy access to cold water line, power outlet, and enough room to change filters.

Technical specifications

Voltage and Frequency: 110V-240V 50Hz/60Hz
Wattage: 25W-36W RO Element
Capacity: 50/100 GPD
Tank Capacity: 12 liters
Plastic Inlet TDS: ≤ 250ppm
Chlorine Level: ≤ 0.2ppm
Average Reverse Osmosis Rejection Rate: 98%
Inlet Water Pressure (Min/Max): 1 – 3 Bar
Inlet Water Temperature (Min/Max): 5°C - 45°C
Flush Type (model dependent): Auto

System maintenance

The following are some guidelines for scheduled filter changes
Change Spun PP Filter every 6 months or as required,
Change RO Membrane every 12-18months or as required,
Change Inline Carbon Filter every 6-12 months or as required, When the unit is not in use for an extended period of time, disconnect it from power supply. Before resuming use, perform the same flushing procedure as in the initial set up.
